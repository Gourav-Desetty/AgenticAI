Game of Thrones and House of the Dragon are set in the fictional continent of Westeros, where noble houses compete for control of the Iron Throne.

House of the Dragon is a prequel to Game of Thrones and focuses on House Targaryen during the reign of King Viserys I Targaryen. Viserys I names his daughter Rhaenyra Targaryen as his heir, breaking tradition favoring male succession. After Viserys dies, a civil war known as the Dance of the Dragons erupts between Rhaenyra Targaryen and her half-brother Aegon II Targaryen, who is supported by Queen Alicent Hightower and House Hightower.

Rhaenyra is supported by Daemon Targaryen, her uncle and later husband. The conflict divides the realm into two factions: the Blacks (supporting Rhaenyra) and the Greens (supporting Aegon II). Dragons play a central role in the war, leading to massive destruction and the eventual decline of Targaryen dragon power.

Approximately 170 years later, Game of Thrones begins with Robert Baratheon as King of the Seven Kingdoms after he defeated the Mad King Aerys II Targaryen during Robert’s Rebellion. Aerys II was the last Targaryen king before the dynasty fell. His children, Viserys Targaryen and Daenerys Targaryen, were exiled to Essos.

House Stark of Winterfell, led initially by Eddard Stark, becomes entangled in political intrigue in King’s Landing. After Ned Stark’s execution by King Joffrey Baratheon (who is secretly the son of Cersei Lannister and Jaime Lannister), the War of the Five Kings begins.

The major houses involved in Game of Thrones include:
- House Stark (North, Winterfell)
- House Lannister (Westerlands, Casterly Rock)
- House Baratheon (Stormlands)
- House Targaryen (formerly ruling dynasty)
- House Greyjoy (Iron Islands)
- House Tyrell (Reach)

Daenerys Targaryen eventually rises in Essos, hatching three dragons: Drogon, Rhaegal, and Viserion. She conquers cities such as Astapor and Meereen before sailing to Westeros to reclaim the Iron Throne.

Jon Snow, believed to be the bastard son of Ned Stark, is later revealed to be Aegon Targaryen, the son of Rhaegar Targaryen and Lyanna Stark. This makes him both a Stark and a Targaryen, linking the two bloodlines central to both series.

The Night King and the White Walkers threaten all of Westeros from beyond the Wall, forcing temporary alliances between rival houses. The final season concludes with Bran Stark becoming King of the Six Kingdoms after Daenerys is killed by Jon Snow to prevent further destruction.

In summary:
- House of the Dragon focuses on Targaryen civil war (Dance of the Dragons).
- Game of Thrones focuses on political fragmentation after Targaryen collapse.
- Dragons symbolize Targaryen power in both eras.
- Succession disputes drive both conflicts.
- The Stark and Targaryen bloodlines intersect through Jon Snow.
